Sri Lanka Squeeze

Hand-crafted Travel Experiences
No of Days: 8
No of Nights: 7

  • Kandy City Walk

  • Tea Plantation & Tea Factory Visit

Day 1

You will be met on arrival at the Bandaranaike International Airport, Colombo by our representative who will assist you with your luggage and guide you to your vehicle.

Drive to Kandy, visiting a spice plantation en route

Kandy the historic city was known as “Kanda Uda Rata”, which means the hill country surrounded by mountains was located about 480m above sea level. Also a UNESCO world heritage site. Kandy was home to Sri Lanka's last independent Kingdom, which survived two centuries of colonial incursion before finally falling at the beginning of the nineteenth century to the British. Visit the Kandy Tooth Relic Temple which is known as “Sri Dalada Maligawa” consists the Sacred Tooth of Lord Gautama Buddha. The entire complex is at least over three centuries old.

On completion check in at Hotel

Overnight stay at the Oak Ray Heritage or Similar Hotel

Day 2

Breakfast at the Hotel

After breakfast at the hotel, we will attend to a Sri Lankan cultural show. Then a guided city walks in Kandy with smart access to Peradeniya Botanical Gardens which is the largest (60 hectares) and most impressive botanic gardens in Sri Lanka. Include a fine collection of royal palms, orchids, cannonball fruit tree, 40m-high Burma bamboo and giant Javan fig tree with umbrella-like canopy of branches. You also can experience the hundreds of monkeys and the gardens with thousands of wing-flapping fruit bats.

Kandy is also famous for its gems and jewellery, shopping, handicrafts, silks, batiks etc. And you will have the opportunity of visit to your favourable heart’s content with the memorable view of the picturesque town from Upper Lake Drive.

On completion return to Hotel

Overnight stay at the Oak Ray Heritage or Similar Hotel

Day 3

Breakfast at the Kandy Hotel, you will proceed to Nuwara Eliya by passing Tea Plantations.

Visiting a tea plantation & factory en route where the world’s best tea is produced. Here you could observe all about the process of tea manufacturing and how the manufactured Ceylon Tea is graded. Also you can taste a cup of pure Ceylon Tea.

Drive to Nuwara Eliya, City Tour. Enjoy high tea in Grand Hotel Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a city in the Central Mountain Range of Sri Lanka also known as ‘Little’ England’. Attractive with the breathtaking views of valleys, salubrious climate, meadows, mountains and greenery. Nuwara Eliya meaning "the city of dawn" is the city at the highest elevation (2000m above sea level) in Sri Lanka and its tallest peak, Piduruthalagala (2555m) could be also seen from here. The city established by the British in the nineteenth century and still having the monuments like old brick Post office, little bungalows etc. Apart from that you can also enjoy with race course, 18 hole golf course, walk around Gregory lake and your mindful attractions in and around the heart of the tea country in Sri Lanka.

On completion check in at Hotel

Overnight stay at the Oak Ray Summer Breeze or Similar Hotel

Day 4

Breakfast at the Hotel

Catch the train to Ella which is considered as one of the most scenic train journeys in the world. This takes you through beautiful mountain views, stunning landscapes of green and lush forests, tea plantations, waterfalls, old bridges and calm small villages.

On completion check in at Hotel

Overnight stay at the Oak Ray Ella Gap or Similar Hotel

Day 5

Breakfast at the Hotel

After the scenic train trip to Ella, the next stage is Elle Rock hiking. It is a 3 hours hike for the whole journey. You will be greeted with breathtaking views once you reach the peak; rolling green valleys, the sun rising higher, lots mountain ranges around with beautiful waterfalls.

Next glamorous hike is the Little Adams Peak which is on a cross country hike across to Demodara Railway Station, can see the extraordinary perspectives from the summit of the peak. Stroll over the well known Nine Arch Bridge with its widespread history. Also called as the Bridge in the Sky and giving best example for the Sri Lanka’s colonial-era railway constructions. This historical construction was built by P. K. Appuhami with the help of British engineer’s consultation. Climb to the Demodara Railroad Station and see the interesting and unique looping railroad, a bit of fabulous architecture that is just present at Demodara in Sri Lanka.

On completion return to Hotel

Overnight stay at the Oak Ray Ella Gap or Similar Hotel

Day 6

Breakfast at the Hotel

Drive to Udawalewe, Safari in Udawalewe National Park, Elephant Transit Home

Udawalawe National Park, one of the largest animal sanctuary in Sri Lanka. This was established in the year 1972 to protect the catchment area of Udawalawe reservoir that provides water resource for agricultural purposes and hydro-power generation. This is the best national park in Sri Lanka that you can watch giant Asian wild elephants throughout the year. There are hundreds of elephants in the park and approximately its 500. Apart from the elephants you have the opportunity to see other animals in the park including samber, spotted deer, sambur deer, water buffalo, wild boar, bandicoots, jackal, mongooses, foxes, gray langers, leopards and many more while hanging on our safari. We hope for sure this is the best experience you get from a safari ride in Sri Lanka.

Elephant Transit Home in Udawalewe was established in 1995 by the support of Born Free Foundation in UK. This is caring house for orphaned elephants in Sri Lanka. After the successful rehabilitation, the matured elephants are released back into their natural wild habitat, many of them into the Udawalawe National Park some are for the other national parks in Sri Lanka. You can enjoy with their feeding time, not close to the elephants but from a viewing platform. Unlike in other orphanages the good thing in here is the elephants are not chained at night.

On completion check in at Hotel

Overnight stay at the Hotel

Day 7

Breakfast at the Hotel, you will proceed to Galle & guided visit to Galle Dutch Fortres

Galle is the administrative capital of Sri Lankan Southern Province. This is a historical city which is a Unesco World Heritage Site that enriched with Dutch colonial buildings, museums, ancient churches and mosques, grand mansions, rambling lanes etc.

Galle Dutch Fortress is a world heritage site located in the bay of Galle in Sri Lanka. This was first built by the Portuguese in the year 1588 and next by the Dutch in the period of 17th century, nearly in 1649. This is an archaeological and architectural monument that brought up more than 432 years of history which maintains an attractive appearance, by the Archaeological Department of Sri Lanka with their extensive reconstruction work process. Keep in mind Galle Dutch Fortress is the largest remaining fortress in all around Asia built by Europeans and you can explore the fortress many more with our professional tour guides.
