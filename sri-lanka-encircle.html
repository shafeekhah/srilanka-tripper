Day 1

You will be met on arrival at the Bandaranaike International Airport, Colombo by our representative who will assist you with your luggage and guide you to your vehicle.

Thereafter proceed to Negombo to visit Negombo fish market. Negombo is located on the west coast of Sri Lanka, between the Colombo and the International Airport and well known for its lagoon, Dutch built canal system and delicious seafood. The Negombo fish market, which is one of the largest fish market in Sri Lanka. Every day, fishers take their outrigger canoes to the deep sea to come up with large amount of fish to the market. You can enjoy such daily fish auctions with how they sell fish to locals in “Lellama”.

Next you can try the fascinating Dutch Canel Boat Tour. The Canal is also known as Hamilton Canal which was built by the British, started in the year 1802 and ended up in 1804. During the time of colonization this was functioned as a supply waterway to the Dutch administration. Today this use as travel and transport purposes and for remarkable boat tours. Visitors can explore the beautiful views and natural habitats via 14km of Dutch canal boat safari, also tour can be prolonged by following the canal in to the Negombo Sea.

On completion check in at Hotel

Overnight stay at the Sea Horse or Similar Hotel

Day 2

Breakfast at the Negombo Hotel, you will next proceed to Sigiriya

Sigiriya Rock Fortress Visit is one of the greatest experience you will get during the tour.

Sigiriya is a historical and archaeologically famous massive column of rock located in Dambulla which is nearly 200 metres high. The rock was chosen by the King Kashyapa for his new capital in between 477 - 495 AD and he built the fortress in Sigiriya while the palace was on the top of the rock. Sigiriya the name comes from attractive Lion shaped structure called as the Lion Rock, also this is enriched with colourful frescoes and magical water springs that brings water bottom to top of the rock without any human involvement. After the king’s death the palace and the fortress was abandoned and afterwards until 14th century it was a Buddhist monastery. You can clearly explore the ancient urban planning and today Sigiriya is a UNESCO World Heritage.

On completion check in at Hotel

Overnight stay at the Fresco Water Villa or Similar Hotel

Day 3

Breakfast at the Hotel

Sigiriya Village Tour is the next episode in your journey. This gives you an inspirational experience regarding the Sri Lankan tradition and village hospitality. Villagers are incorporate with many different activities in their day to day life style. You can also engage with those activities like a catamaran ride by enjoying nature, an oxen cart ride (the primary mode of transport commonly used in Sri Lankan villages), a walk through the fields including seasonal paddy cultivations & other essential crops and spend time with villagers to understand their authenticate life styles. The best thing is you will get a perfect learning about how to cook traditional Sri Lankan food and finally served you a delicious buffet lunch with classic Sri Lankan style. This will be perfectly cooked rice and curries including green salad and crunchy Papadam. The food will be cooked in clay pots over wood fires and served on clean lotus or banana leaves as a village tradition in Sri Lanka.

After the lunch you will proceed to Mineriya or Kaudulla national park for a jeep safari to see hundreds of elephant gatherings at tank banks and grass fields. Some evidences prove that the number of elephants to as high as 700. Rather than that, the safari is somewhat adventural to see animals like leopards. Also you will be able to see other animals like monkeys, dears, many kinds of birds etc.

On completion return to Hotel

Overnight stay at the Fresco Water Villa or Similar Hotel

Day 4

Breakfast at the Hotel, you will next proceed to Kandy the Hill Country

Drive to Kandy, visiting a spice plantation en route to get noticed about the Ayurvedic Herbal Spices in Sri Lanka. You will also be able to test flavors of some spices like pepper, cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, vanilla etc.

Kandy the historic city was known as “Kanda Uda Rata”, which means the hill country surrounded by mountains was located about 480m above sea level. Also an UNESCO world heritage site. Kandy was home to Sri Lanka's last independent Kingdom, which survived two centuries of colonial incursion before finally falling at the beginning of the nineteenth century to the British. Visit the Kandy Tooth Relic Temple which is known as “Sri Dalada Maligawa” consists the Sacred Tooth of Lord Gautama Buddha. This is the most sacred and venerated Buddhist temple in Sri Lanka. The entire complex is at least over three centuries old.

On completion check in at Hotel

Overnight stay at the Heritage or Similar Hotel

Day 5

Breakfast at the Hotel

After breakfast at the hotel, we will attend to a Sri Lankan cultural show. Then a guided city walks in Kandy with smart access to Peradeniya Botanical Gardens which is the largest (60 hectares) and most impressive botanic garden in Sri Lanka. Include a fine collection of royal palms, orchids, cannonball fruit tree, 40m-high Burma bamboo and giant Javan fig tree with umbrella-like canopy of branches. You also can experience the hundreds of monkeys and the gardens with thousands of wing-flapping fruit bats.

Kandy is also famous for its gems and jewellery, shopping, handicrafts, silks, batiks etc. And you will have the opportunity of visit to your favourable heart’s content with the memorable view of the picturesque town from Upper Lake Drive.

On completion return to Hotel

Day 6

Breakfast at the Hotel

Breakfast at the Kandy Hotel, you will proceed to Nuwara Eliya by passing Tea Plantations.

Visiting a tea plantation & factory en route where the world’s best tea is produced. Here you could observe all about the process of tea manufacturing and how the manufactured Ceylon Tea is graded. Also you can taste a cup of pure Ceylon Tea.

Drive to Nuwara Eliya, City Tour.

Nuwara Eliya is a city in the Central Mountain Range of Sri Lanka also known as ‘Little’ England’. Attractive with the breathtaking views of valleys, salubrious climate, meadows, mountains and greenery. Nuwara Eliya meaning "the city of dawn" is the city at the highest elevation (2000m above sea level) in Sri Lanka and its tallest peak, Piduruthalagala (2555m) could be also seen from here. The city established by the British in the nineteenth century and still having the monuments like old brick Post office, little bungalows etc. Victoria Park which is best maintained public park in the city center with number of flowers, trees and bird species. Apart from that you can also enjoy with race course, 18 hole golf course, walk around Gregory lake and your mindful attractions in and around the heart of the tea country in Sri Lanka.

Day 7

Breakfast at the Hotel

Hortain Plain Treaking in the morning. The Horton Plains is a National park in the central highlands of Sri Lanka mostly covered with montane grasslands. The plateau comes to an end at a stunning view of “World’s End”. This is an escarpment that plunges 880m. The walk journey from entrance to the End is 4km, rather way there is Baker’s Falls within 2km away which is a cool and beautiful place and then back to the entrance adding another 3.5km of walk. All together its around 10km journey that takes three to four hours. But we hope for sure you are getting an utmost experience within these 4 hours. The weather in here change very quickly and its good to visit early in the morning to capture the breathtaking views of World’s End. Unless, the mist and clouds will roll in.

Next you can enjoy high tea in Grand Hotel Nuwara Eliya. the Grand Tea Lounge in hotel offers a delightful wood fired Pizzas, cakes, snacks, sandwiches with a vast selection of different teas in Sri Lanka. This is a memorable one for sure in your tour in Nuwara Eliya.

Breakfast at the Hotel, scenic train trip to Ella

Catch the train to Ella which is considered as one of the most scenic train journeys in the world. This takes you through beautiful mountain views, stunning landscapes of green and lush forests, tea plantations, waterfalls, old bridges and calm small villages.

Little Adams Peak for Sunset, Little Adams Peak which is on a cross country hike across to Demodara Railway Station, can see the extraordinary perspectives from the summit of the peak. You can capture a nice and shine sunset while on the peak on the little Adams Peak that brings a fresh evening for next morning tour.

Day 9

Breakfast at the Hotel

Next glamorous hike is Elle Rock hiking. It is a 3 hours hike for the whole journey. You will be greeted with breathtaking views once you reach the peak; rolling green valleys, the sun rising higher, lots of mountain ranges around with beautiful waterfalls.

Stroll over the well known Nine Arch Bridge with its widespread history. Also called as the Bridge in the Sky and giving best example for the Sri Lanka’s colonial-era railway constructions. This historical construction was built by P. K. Appuhami with the help of British engineer’s consultation. Climb to the Demodara Railroad Station and see the interesting and unique looping railroad, a bit of fabulous architecture that is just present at Demodara in Sri Lanka.

Day 10

Breakfast at the Hotel, Drive to Yala National Park

Yala is the Sri Lanka's most famous and huge national park. This huge forest which bordered the Indian Ocean contains total area of 1268 sq km of scrub, grasslands and brackish lagoons. Park is having such a beautiful natural habitat for wildlife animals such as elephants, leopards, buffaloes, crocodiles and monkeys also with hundreds of bird species.

Day 11

Breakfast at the Hotel, you will proceed to Galle & guided visit to Galle Dutch Fortress

Galle is the administrative capital of Sri Lankan Southern Province. This is a historical city which is a Unesco World Heritage Site that enriched with Dutch colonial buildings, museums, ancient churches and mosques, grand mansions, rambling lanes etc.

Galle Dutch Fortress is a world heritage site located in the bay of Galle in Sri Lanka. This was first built by the Portuguese in the year 1588 and next by the Dutch in the period of 17th century, nearly in 1649. This is an archaeological and architectural monument that brought up more than 432 years of history which maintains an attractive appearance, by the Archaeological Department of Sri Lanka with their extensive reconstruction work process. Keep in mind Galle Dutch Fortress is the largest remaining fortress in all around Asia built by Europeans and you can explore the fortress many more with our professional tour guides.
